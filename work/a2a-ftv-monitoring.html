FTV Monitoring — a unified Control Room for A2A's solar plants

Redesigning real-time monitoring for A2A Calore e Servizi's photovoltaic installations — replacing fragmented, vendor-locked tools with one Control Room for backoffice, maintenance teams and clients.

RoleDesign Lead (PwC)
ClientA2A — Calore e Servizi
PlatformWeb & Mobile
PhaseDiscovery → Design & Prototype

The challenge

ACS (A2A Calore e Servizi) manages photovoltaic, district heating and lighting systems for public administrations and municipalities across Italy, under contracts that vary widely in duration and scope. Over the years, ACS had tested several disconnected monitoring platforms — Wiser, Easy Everywhere, and various inverter-brand tools (Samsung, Coster, Trilux) — each tied to a specific hardware vendor.

None of them could show aggregated data across multiple plants at once. This was a real pain point for managing energy communities in particular, which required heavy manual work to reconcile disaggregated data. ACS wanted a single, proprietary "control room" that any operator could use to monitor every site, regardless of which hardware was installed.

What the team set out to achieve

  • Reduce operational time for the backoffice team
  • Reduce the time to restore a plant to normal operation after a fault
  • Reduce the time to handle tickets and maintenance requests
  • Give clients both single-plant and aggregated production data
  • Ship a mobile app with alerts, not just a web dashboard
  • Build toward a multi-service energy-efficiency platform, not just solar

Process

The project follows a Design Thinking double-diamond approach, run in two-week Scrum sprints with daily standups, sprint planning/review and dedicated co-design sessions with ACS stakeholders. As of this write-up, the team has completed the Discovery & Setup phase and is moving into Design & Prototype.

1

Setup & Discovery workshops

Four structured sessions — Setup, Discovery Design, Discovery Tech & Data, and Kick-off — with the Product Owner, Team Owner, Sponsor and technical SMEs, to map the as-is landscape and align on scope.

2

Platform benchmarking

Analysed three reference platforms — the existing EF Monitoring/Easy Everywhere setup, Schneider's Wiser, and Fusion Solar (Huawei) — to inform the information architecture and KPI prioritisation of the new system.

3

User & data modelling

Defined four user profiles (Admin, Backoffice, Manutentore, Cliente) with differentiated access, and mapped the entity relationships between clients, sites, plants, devices, users and documents.

4

Design & Prototype (in progress)

Sprint-based UX/UI design of screens and flows, validated in tech review and design approval sessions before moving to delivery and UAT testing.

The solution

The new FTV Virtual Control Room gives every user type — internal ACS staff, external maintenance technicians and clients — a view scoped to what they actually need.

Admin & Backoffice

Full access to every plant, user and document; the only profiles that can create new admin/backoffice accounts and manage client records.

Manutentore (maintenance)

View-only access to assigned plants and devices, alarms, and the ability to generate reports — no account or content management.

Cliente

Sees only their own plants' production data, documents and (on request) device detail — plus the economic translation of energy data where relevant.

Home page, two ways

A map view with status-coded pins per plant, and a list view combining a cumulative KPI overview with a sortable, customisable plant table.

Alarm severity, kept simple

Alerts are classified into three levels so operators can triage at a glance: Grave (the plant stops or malfunctions), Lieve (a component is faulty but the plant keeps producing), and Avviso (output has dropped due to external factors, e.g. weather).

Mobile, scoped down

The companion mobile app targets clients and maintenance technicians specifically — a deliberately reduced slice of the web platform's information, profiled the same way. See the A2A Insight App case study for that side of the project.
